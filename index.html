Phase 1: Research

Problem

Animal sanctuary struggles with highlighting the difference between what they do as a sanctuary and the services a shelter is confined to. This limits the potential for animals that have been rehomed there to find a forever home beyond the sanctuary.

Audience

Those who want to adopt not shop
Want to help out animals in need
Those looking for a friend

User Research

Our user research helped us find and see many mistakes through out the current website. There were many broken pages, the nav bar awas confusing at some points, Many pages just took you in a circle, and many other alignment issues.

We wanted to fix the following:

  1. Consolidate the site map pages
  2. Help users get to where they want quicker
  3. Keep all users on our site rather than transfering to 3rd party
  4. Fill out applications on our site instead of downloading.

Phase 2: Ideation

Ideation

The process of ideation started by organizing the website by card sorting to simplify the flow and create a sitemap. We then started sketching out wireframes to try different layouts and to also see how to consolidate all the important information to users.

Once the wireframes came together in XD we continued testing and iterating until we were ready to bring the site to life.

Phase 3: Designing

Hi-Fi Wireframes

After the lo-fi wireframes we created a style tile to help us design the hi-fi pages also to make sure the colors complimented one another. We also created a user journey experience to help us envision how a user would use the website/

Once the pages started to all come together we continued to iterate to make sure the all the pages corolated togehter and they were as user friendly as possible.

Final Product

Key Takeaways

Since there were 4 group members, it was difficult to keep consistancy in the beginning, so we definitely learned how to work better as a team. We also wished we took more advantage of user testing so we could have gotten more feedback, our last take away would be to simplify. It's hard not to get caught up in wanting to add and make everything so much more than it needs to be.
